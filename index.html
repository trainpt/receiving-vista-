Set up the receiving program before harvest

Six pieces have to land before the first truck rolls — pack styles in the system, the office admin trained on harvest crew schedules, #FT sticker rolls ordered, the WMS integration module purchased, every truck's QR magnet registered, and the Receiving App deployed to the C66s. The persona map at the bottom of this tab is your who-does-what reference for the rest of the guide.

Office
Pre-Harvest Data

Every retail pack style needs to live in PickTrace

Once Receiving is on, one pack style per piece rate is no longer enough. Each retail variant — every clamshell, bag, or box destined for a specific buyer — needs its own entry in PickTrace so it can map to the right cost code in the WMS at receiving time. Even when piece rates are identical across variants, the variants themselves can't be collapsed.

  • 1
    In the Pack Styles section, download the import template.
  • 2
    Fill in every retail-specific pack style — not generic categories. Bulk import.
  • 3
    Apply piece rates across the new pack styles using the bulk-apply path so you're not editing crew schedules one row at a time.
    Apply piece rate to multiple pack styles at once; apply pack styles across blocks/locations in one pass.
  • 4
    Confirm cost-code mappings with the Controller before harvest opens.
Volume × volume. The office gets nervous about this step because it's pack-style count multiplied by daily-crew-schedule count — the manual path scales badly. The bulk-apply path is the only realistic way to set this up at scale; surface it early.
Crew schedule training is a separate gap. The office admin doing crew schedules has been trained on the non-harvest variant of crew schedules but not on the harvest variant — which is the one that requires selecting the correct pack style per crew per day. Run a hands-on session specifically on harvest crew schedules in addition to the pack-style import. Treat it as its own training event, not a footnote.
Don't ship without this. If pack styles aren't in PickTrace before harvest opens, the WMS write at cold storage either fails or creates malformed records — there's no per-pack mapping to send. Missing pack styles surface as receiving errors, not as setup errors, which makes them slow to debug under harvest pressure.
Harvest Checker
Sticker Spec

Order the #FT sticker rolls separately from existing label rolls

#FT stickers are temporary pallet identifiers applied at loadout in the field. They are not the existing T&P label rolls and they are not the permanent pallet tags printed out of the WMS at cold storage. They exist for one reason: to give every pallet an addressable identifier through the staging yard handoff.

#FT0000001
#FT followed by a sequential number. The prefix is encoded in the barcode but the app strips it for display — internally the pallet is just the numeric portion. Sequential numbers must not repeat across rolls or crews.
  • 1
    Order rolls from the label vendor with the #FT prefix and sequential numbering.
  • 2
    Plan one roll per harvest checker per season — at ~30,000-pallet scale across the operation, that's roughly the right volume per crew.
  • 3
    Confirm with the vendor whether they generate the sequence or whether the customer needs to supply it.
Duplicate numbers fail silently. If two rolls share a number, the second pallet to scan that ID will be treated as a re-scan of the first — counts and lineage will be wrong but no error fires. Confirm uniqueness across every roll, not just within a single roll, before distributing to crews.
Office
Integration Setup

Two modules drive the WMS connection — only one is always required

The customer purchases the WMS modules directly from the WMS vendor. The implementation team owns the PickTrace side of the integration. Confirm with engineering whether a direct API integration will be ready in time for harvest before the customer commits to the FTP module.

Always required

Receiving / Inventory Import module

The WMS-side module that accepts inbound pallet records from PickTrace at receiving time. Required regardless of whether the integration runs over FTP or a direct API. Approximate cost is in the low four figures from the WMS vendor.

Conditional

FTP / Custom Job Setup module

The WMS-side module that handles file-based exchange. Only required if the PickTrace ↔ WMS integration runs over FTP. If a direct API integration is in scope and ships in time for harvest, this module is unnecessary — don't let the customer purchase it on a guess.

Confirm before commit. Get the API status from engineering in writing before the customer commits to the FTP module. If the API ships, the FTP module is wasted budget; if the API slips past harvest, the FTP module is the fallback the customer needs immediately.
Office
Truck Setup

Register every swamper and larger truck before season start

The entire In-the-Field and Staging Yard flows depend on each truck having a QR magnet that's been pre-registered and physically attached. Scanning the magnet on a checker's or yard manager's device auto-populates the load ticket — destination, current site, driver, company, trailer ID — so the driver never has to touch the app and the checker doesn't type anything.

  • 1
    Inventory every active swamper and every larger truck. Confirm the count with the Harvest Manager.
  • 2
    Coordinate with engineering to register each truck and produce its QR magnet. The registration mechanism is owned by the engineering team — get the workflow from them rather than improvising.
  • 3
    Apply the physical magnets to the trucks before the first day of harvest. Place them where a person standing on the ground with a C66 can scan them without climbing.
  • 4
    Walk the placement with the Harvest Manager and a sample C66 — confirm scan range, lighting, and angle work in actual field conditions, not just a parking lot test.
Missing magnets break the design. If a truck shows up without a registered magnet, the checker has to fall back to manual entry on the load-ticket screen — which kills the no-driver-touches-the-app premise and breaks discrepancy tracking downstream because the load ticket gets typed instead of generated. Catch missing magnets in the pre-season walkthrough, not on day one of harvest.

Device Deployment

Get the Receiving App onto every C66 the harvest checkers use

Checkers already run the Field App on managed C66 devices. The Receiving App is a separate app from the same App Store / Play Store — it has to be installed alongside the Field App so the same checker can toggle between both on the same device. The engineering team has a QR-code distribution mechanism for installs at scale.

  • 1
    Confirm device count with the Harvest Manager (~50 C66s at this customer's scale).
  • 2
    Coordinate with engineering for the QR-code install link.
  • 3
    Walk a representative checker through the install on one device. Once that works, the customer can roll it out across the fleet themselves.
  • 4
    Confirm checkers can toggle between Field App and Receiving App on the same device without re-authenticating each time.
User accounts before installs. Receiving App authentication is email/password at the user level — not the badge-based login of the Field App. Every checker who will use Receiving needs a user account created before the install rollout. Plan account provisioning alongside the device deployment, not after.

T&P Unchanged

T&P stays exactly as it was

Checkers continue to check employees in, pair them to label rolls, and run T&P exactly as they have. The Field App workflow is untouched.

No field tickets are created in the Field App. This is the most important reframing for anyone coming from prior PickTrace deployments: in this customer's setup, "field ticket" is a Receiving-App concept tied to the #FT sticker on a pallet, not a Field-App concept tied to a piece-rate group. If you're trained on the Field-App field-ticket flow from another account, set that mental model aside before reading the rest of this tab.

Harvest Checker
Truck Arrival

Every swamper has a QR magnet

The QR magnet pre-registered to the truck/driver assignment is what kicks off a load ticket. The driver doesn't open the app. The checker scans the magnet on the swamper, the load ticket auto-populates with destination, current site, driver, company, and trailer ID, and the checker taps Save and Continue.

  • 1
    Open the Receiving App on the C66, tap New.
  • 2
    Scan the QR magnet on the swamper. The logistics screen fills in.
  • 3
    Confirm the auto-populated fields, tap Save and Continue.
Auto-fill, not retype. Once a magnet has been scanned for a given truck, driver/company/trailer auto-populate on subsequent scans — the checker confirms, doesn't retype. If a value is wrong, the magnet registration upstream is wrong; flag it rather than editing in the load ticket.
Harvest Checker
Loadout

Apply a #FT sticker, scan the sticker, scan a case, enter box count

This is the dual-scan flow. It exists specifically because the staging yard handoff needs an addressable pallet identifier — without a sticker on the pallet, there's nothing to scan when pallets move from one truck to another. Every pallet gets the same four-step treatment as it goes onto the swamper.

  • 1
    Apply a #FT sticker to the pallet as it's being loaded onto the swamper.
  • 2
    In the Receiving App, scan the #FT sticker.
  • 3
    Scan any random case on that same pallet. The system uses the case label to backfill production data — block, variety, pack style, crew.
  • 4
    Enter the total box count on the pallet when prompted.
    This becomes the field-credited count that the cold-storage receiver will reconcile against later.

This dual-scan flow is distinct from the single-reference-scan flow used by some other table-grape customers. Don't mix the two — the workflows look similar but produce different downstream data.

Confirm the build is shipped. The dual-scan UI is being built by the engineering team specifically for this customer's flow. Verify the build is in the deployed app before training rollout — otherwise the second scan won't be accepted and the field ticket won't create.
Harvest Checker
Departure

Sign off, mark In Transit, swamper leaves

When the swamper is loaded, the truck driver signs off on the checker's device. The checker signs off. The checker then manually moves the load from Loading to In Transit before the truck pulls away.

  • 1
    Driver signs off on the checker's C66.
  • 2
    Checker signs off.
  • 3
    Checker long-presses the load and changes status from Loading to In Transit.
In Transit fires twice in this customer's lifecycle. Once here, when the swamper leaves the field. Once again at the staging yard, when the larger truck leaves for cold storage. Both transitions are manual and both feed the office-tab transit timer. Surface this twice-fired pattern in training so neither the checker nor the yard manager assumes the other handled it.

Staging Yard Manager
The Handoff

Scan each #FT pallet sticker as it moves trucks

As the forklift moves each pallet from the swamper to the larger truck, the manager scans the #FT sticker on the pallet. The system recognizes the pallet is currently on the swamper load and prompts to move it to the new larger-truck load. Confirm; the pallet flips trucks. Pallets can only live on one load at a time — this enforces correctness without any manual reconciliation.

  • 1
    Forklift moves a pallet from the swamper toward the larger truck.
  • 2
    Manager scans the #FT sticker on that pallet.
  • 3
    App prompts: "This pallet is on the swamper load. Move to the larger truck load?" — confirm.
  • 4
    Repeat for every pallet leaving the swamper.
  • 5
    When the larger truck is loaded, get the driver to sign off, sign off as yard manager, and move the larger truck's load from Loading to In Transit.
Two-truck splits are common at peak. If the larger truck fills up before the swamper is empty, repeat §3.3 and §3.4 with a second larger truck for the remaining pallets. Both larger trucks become independent loads heading to cold storage. The original swamper load stays Received at the staging yard regardless of how many larger trucks the pallets split across.
Second In Transit fire. The transition you trigger in step 5 is the second In Transit event of this customer's lifecycle (the first was the swamper leaving the field). Both feed the office timeline.

Cold-Storage Receiver
Access Model

Third-party staff, customer's WMS account

Forklift drivers at the cold-storage facility don't work for the customer — they work for the third-party packer/shipper. They get user accounts on PickTrace (email and password — Receiving App auth is user-level, not badge-based) scoped to the receiving site only. When they print permanent pallet tags from the WMS at receiving time, they're logged into the customer's WMS instance, not their employer's. The pallet records land in the customer's WMS account.

  • 1
    The customer's Controller owns WMS account setup and per-user provisioning on the WMS side.
  • 2
    PickTrace user accounts (email + password) are created for each third-party forklift driver who needs Receiving access.
  • 3
    Each account is destination-scoped to the cold-storage receiving site only — no badge needed.
Destination scope, not org-wide. Scope these accounts so they only see loads inbound to the cold-storage receiving site. Org-wide visibility would surface unrelated loads and create noise the third-party team doesn't need to see.
Multi-party setup. The Controller (customer) and the Cold-Storage Manager (third-party) need to align on which accounts to provision before training rolls out. Coordination here is the slow path — don't leave it for the day before harvest.
Cold-Storage Receiver
Receiving Flow

One truck scan, one scan per pallet

The cold-storage flow mirrors the field flow at the entry point — scan the truck's QR magnet to pull up the load — and then diverges into a per-pallet flow with discrepancy capture. Every pallet gets scanned individually as it comes off the truck.

  • 1
    Forklift driver scans the truck's QR magnet — the load and all its pallets pull up in the Receiving App.
  • 2
    As each pallet comes off, scan its #FT sticker. This marks the pallet received and unlocks the box-count edit field.
  • 3
    If the actual count doesn't match what the field credited, enter the actual count when prompted. (See §4.3.)
  • 4
    Once every pallet is scanned, move the load from In Transit to Received. This transition triggers the WMS write — pallets are created in the WMS in the customer's account.
  • 5
    From the WMS, bulk-print the permanent pallet tags. Apply them to the pallets — either stick over the temporary #FT sticker or rip the temp sticker off and replace. Either approach works.
Per-pallet, not bulk. A shortcut "count-them-all-and-mark-received" path exists in the app. Discourage it. Per-pallet scanning is the only way to capture box-count discrepancies — and discrepancies are the customer's primary value driver for adopting Receiving in the first place. Skipping the per-pallet step throws away the ROI.
Setup gates this stage. The WMS integration must be live and every retail pack style must be set up in PickTrace (Stage 1) before the first cold-storage scan. Otherwise the WMS write at step 4 either fails outright or creates malformed records that have to be reconciled by hand.
Cold-Storage Receiver
Box Count Mismatch

Surface mismatches at scan time, not week-end

Discrepancies get captured here, in real time, as pallets are received. The receiver isn't being asked to investigate gaps — just to enter what's actually on the pallet when it doesn't match what the field credited. The Invoice Auditor Team (Stage 5) works the queue from there.

Worked example. Field credited 65 boxes on a pallet. Receiver counts 40. Receiver enters 40. The 25-box gap is flagged on the office Discrepancy page automatically — no paperwork, no follow-up call. The Invoice Auditor Team picks it up and investigates the source: under-credit at loadout, short-pack from the crew, loss in transit, or something else.
Count, don't just scan. The third-party receiving team needs to understand the expectation explicitly: they're being asked to count, not just scan. If they default to scan-and-move, the discrepancy data is empty and the office tab loses the value-add. Surface this in third-party training, not just customer training.

Discrepancy

Catch box-count mismatches before they hit inventory

The Discrepancy page surfaces gaps between field-credited and receiver-counted box counts at the field-ticket (per-pallet) level. The Invoice Auditor Team owns this view. Receiving feeds sellable inventory directly — errors here have downstream sales impact.

Inventory impact. The receiving process feeds directly into what the customer can sell. Don't disrupt this process at peak — fruit needs to reach the cooler as fast as possible. Build the auditor workflow around catching mismatches without slowing the receiving floor down.
Worked example.

— Receiver counted: 40 boxes
— Gap: 25 boxes

Office
Unit Search

Trace any unit back to the field

Scan or paste any unit ID — case barcode, #FT sticker, anything with a unique ID — to pull the full production record: the employee who picked it, the crew, pick time, and any notes. If receiving counted 40 but the production record shows 65, the discrepancy is immediately visible at the unit level. Notes added here stay attached to the record and resurface the next time the unit is scanned.

Load Summary

Share a load via QR code or PDF

Long-press a load and select QR Code or Download PDF. The QR code can be scanned by a receiver to pull the load up instantly; alternatively, every receiving device automatically shows all loads inbound to its destination, so no QR is strictly needed for the standard arrival path.

The PDF surfaces logistics at the top — driver, employer, crews represented, destination — and below that the total field-ticket count with same-pack groupings collapsed and variants broken out. Useful as a paper trail or hand-off artifact when devices aren't available.
